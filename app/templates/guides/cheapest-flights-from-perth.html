    Perth Airport (PER) has a unique position among Australian airports — it's the closest Australian city to Europe and Asia by geography, yet historically had fewer direct options than Sydney or Melbourne. That has changed significantly in recent years. Perth now offers one of the world's longest direct flights (to London Heathrow) and strong connections through Singapore, Dubai, and Doha, making it a surprisingly competitive departure point for international travel.

    Perth to Bali: Australia's Best-Value Short-Haul Route

    Perth to Bali is the shortest international flight of any Australian capital city to a major holiday destination — just 3.5 hours. The combination of short flying time, multiple competing carriers, and high frequency keeps fares competitive. It's common to find Perth–Bali return fares under AUD $400, and flash sales occasionally push this below AUD $300.

    The key to getting the lowest fare is acting fast when sales appear. Garuda Indonesia, Jetstar, and Scoot all run periodic sales, often tied to school holiday periods or airline promotional events. These fares typically disappear within 48 hours.

    Perth's geographic advantage: Perth sits significantly closer to Asia than Sydney or Melbourne — about 2 hours less flying time to Singapore, and 3.5 hours less to Bali. For frequent travellers to Asia, Perth-based monitoring is worth doing independently from east coast routes.

    Direct to London: Is the Qantas Non-Stop Worth It?

    The Qantas PER–LHR direct flight (QF9/QF10) is the world's third longest commercial route by distance and takes approximately 17 hours. Whether it's worth the premium over a one-stop option depends on how you value your time and comfort.

    • Direct fares typically run AUD $300–800 more than the cheapest one-stop option via Singapore or Dubai.
    • One-stop via Emirates (DXB) is often the best-value alternative, with fares that can undercut Singapore Airlines significantly.
    • One-stop via Singapore Airlines (SIN) is popular for its in-flight product and competitive pricing on sale fares.

    Monitoring both the direct Qantas route and a via-hub alternative is a smart strategy — when a sale on the non-stop drops the premium to under AUD $200, it's often worth taking.

    Best Time to Book Flights from Perth

    The same general rule applies as for other Australian cities: book 6 weeks to 3 months out for international travel. Perth's peak travel periods are slightly different to the east coast:

    • Bali: Year-round demand, but cheapest in April–May and September–October (avoiding school holidays and Bali's peak tourist season).
    • Europe: April and May (autumn in Australia) offer the best combination of cheap fares and good European weather. Avoid July–August.
    • Singapore/KL: Relatively stable pricing year-round. Sales are common — watch for them rather than trying to time the market.
